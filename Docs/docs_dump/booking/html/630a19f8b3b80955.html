Completing the Know Your Partner (KYP) form

Updated 2 weeks ago
7 min read

In this article, you’ll learn how to verify the information needed to complete the Know Your Partner (KYP) form, as well as how to complete the form itself.

To comply with regulatory requirements, we (including our group companies) need to collect and verify specific information from our partners. We do this through the KYP form. You can find the KYP form in the Extranet’s compliance center, which can only be accessed by primary account holders.

If you’ve already completed the Know Your Partner (KYP) form but are still receiving reminders, it could be because your properties have different Legal Entity ID numbers. Each Legal Entity ID is required to have its own KYP form.

Information required for business entities

If you’re completing the Know Your Partner (KYP) form as a business entity, we’ll ask you to provide:

  • Business info
  • Director and ultimate beneficial owner information
  • Bank account details
  • Business structure.
  • Full legal name as it appears on the business registration document (if the name that’s prefilled in the KYP form is incorrect, you can indicate this in the form and you’ll be directed to update your Accommodation Agreement with the correct name). 
  • Business registration number (if applicable).
  • Name of trade register your business is registered in (if applicable).
  • Registered business address (not the address of your property).
  • Tax Identification Number (TIN) and issuing country
  • VAT number and issuing country.
  • Public contact info that's displayed on your property listings.

Depending on whether you use our payment solutions, you might also be asked to provide additional information specifically for Booking Holdings Financial Services (BHFS) in the  KYP form. This information includes, but isn’t limited to:

  • Details of the individual(s) who owns 25% or more of the total shares, voting rights, or other equity of the business entity (i.e. ultimate beneficial owners).
  • Details of the individuals who manage and run the company (i.e. directors).

We’ll need to verify at least one person who manages or directs your business. The individuals you've identified as directors in the Know Your Partner (KYP) form will be asked to submit the following information:

  • Proof of identity – depending on your location, this is usually a government ID, driver's license, or passport. 
  • Proof of address – this document needs to have been issued within the last six months. It can be a utility bill, credit card statement, or bank statement. You’ll need to provide the full document, but you can block out sensitive information such as utility costs, bank account numbers, balances, and transactions.

If you enabled Payments by Booking.com with bank transfer payouts, you’ll need to provide your bank account details to comply with regulatory requirements and to receive payouts for your reservations. 

Here’s how to add or edit bank details:

  1. Sign in to the Extranet.
  2. Click the Finance menu, then Finance settings.
  3. Under Receiving payouts from Booking.com, click Edit bank details to update your information.
  4. Click Save.

If you manage multiple properties, be sure to check the Bank details page through the Finance menu in the group Extranet.

Verification documents required for business entities

If you’re a business entity and we can’t automatically verify your details, you’ll need to provide us with proof of business. This document must include the following info:

  • Legal business name.
  • Registered business address (not your personal residential address or the address of your property).
  • Business registration number.
If several documents are listed for your country, you only need to provide one.

Information required for individuals

If you’re completing the Know Your Partner (KYP) form as an individual, we’ll ask you to provide:

  • Personal information
  • Bank account details
  • Full name as it appears on your passport or ID (if the name that’s prefilled in the KYP form is incorrect, you can indicate this in the form, and you’ll be directed to update your Accommodation Agreement with the correct name).
  • Primary residential address (not the address of your property).
  • Date of birth
  • Place of birth.
  • Tax Identification Number (TIN) and issuing country
  • Value-Added Tax (VAT) number and issuing country (if applicable).
  • An email and phone number that guests can use to contact you (this will be displayed on your property page).
  • A photo of your government-issued identity document (depending on your country, this is usually a government ID, driver's license or passport).
  • Proof of address (this document needs to have been issued within the last six months. It can be either a utility bill, credit card statement, or bank statement. You have to provide the full document so we can see what type it is. However, you can block out sensitive information like utility costs, bank account numbers, balances, and transactions. It needs to show your residential address, not the address of your property).

If any of this information has already been collected previously, we’ll pre-populate it in the form.

If you enabled Payments by Booking.com with bank transfer payouts, you’ll need to provide your bank account details to comply with regulatory requirements and to receive payouts for your reservations. 

Here’s how to add or edit bank details:

  1. Sign in to the Extranet.
  2. Click the Finance menu, then Finance settings.
  3. Under Receiving payouts from Booking.com, click Edit bank details to update your information.
  4. Click Save.

If you manage multiple properties, be sure to check the Bank details page through the Finance menu in the group Extranet.

Tasks you may need complete before filling in the Know Your Partner (KYP) form

To ensure that you’re set up to complete the KYP form, we may prompt you to take some additional steps.

Setting up a primary account

To access the KYP form, you must have a primary account. Only the primary account holder has the relevant permissions to access all properties belonging to a Legal Entity Agreement. 

Updating Accommodation Agreement details

We’ll need to collect and verify the KYP for each Legal Entity ID (LEID) – i.e. each business or individual that signed an accommodation agreement with us. This means that your Accommodation Agreement will need to be updated with partner-level information, rather than the property-level information we’ve collected in the past.

We may ask you to review and update the agreement details before completing the KYP form. Your updated details will be included in the KYP form for validation.

Verifying your portfolio

We’ll ask you to fill out a KYP form that covers all the properties included in your Accommodation Agreement. Before doing so, you’ll need to verify your portfolio to make sure it includes all properties.

If necessary, we’ll ask you to check your properties in the compliance center through the portfolio management tool. If your portfolio isn’t accurate, you can: 

  • Remove a property – this will permanently close and terminate the contract with us for that property
  • Change the ownership of a property – you should only do this if the contracting party (i.e. the individual or the business that signed the Accommodation Agreement with us) changes
  • Add a property from another LEID – to do this, you need to have access to the primary accounts of both the original and the new LEID.
